Dr. John Garang De Mabior

President Of Southern Sudan

Life and Educational background

  • 1945: Dr. John Garang De Mabior was born on June 23, 1945, in Buk village, Sudan (now South Sudan).
  • 1953-1962: He received his primary education at the village school in Buk.
  • 1962-1968: Garang attended Rumbek Secondary School in Rumbek, South Sudan.
  • 1968-1970: He pursued his higher education at the University of Khartoum in Sudan, where he obtained a Bachelor of Arts degree in Economics.
  • 1970-1972: Garang joined the Sudanese army and underwent military training in Sudan's Military Academy.
  • 1972-1976: He continued his military education in the United States, attending the United States Army Infantry School in Fort Benning, Georgia.
  • 1976-1977: Garang completed his military training at the United States Army Command and General Staff College in Fort Leavenworth, Kansas.
  • 1977-1983: He furthered his education by pursuing a Master's degree in Agricultural Economics at Iowa State University in Ames, Iowa.
  • 1983-2005: Throughout his career as a military officer and political leader, Garang continued to expand his knowledge and expertise through various training programs and courses, both within Sudan and internationally.
